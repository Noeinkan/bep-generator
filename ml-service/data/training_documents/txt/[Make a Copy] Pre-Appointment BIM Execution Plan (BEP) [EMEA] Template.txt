Template governance
This Pre-Appointment BIM Execution Plan (BEP) template will be reviewed and updated from time to time to support continual development and change within Google. Trigger events to initiate review and update shall be, but not limited to those contained within the table below. Google shall assess impact of changes to the BIM Execution Plan, with consideration of the downstream influence on current projects.
GUIDANCE
Google has provided the ‘Pre-Appointment BIM Execution Plan template’ to support Prospective Lead Appointed Party’s, and their prospective Delivery Team.
The aim of providing a Pre-Appointment BIM Execution Plan template is to enable consistency.

Where notes appear like [THIS], the author of the Pre-Appointment BIM Execution Plan should input the relevant information.

1.1	Who uses the BIM Execution Plan?
The Pre-appointment BIM Execution Plan shall be used by the prospective Lead Appointed Party and Delivery Team to communicate to Google their proposed approach to producing, managing and exchanging information. This enables Google to determine the suitability of the prospective Lead-Appointing Party and their Delivery Team against the project’s information requirements.
If appointed, the Pre-appointment BIM Execution Plan will be updated to confirm the approach.

Only software identified below shall be used to author, coordinate and validate information models. Note:
Project stakeholders authoring information models shall allow for full version updates on a repeating two-year cycle, e.g Revit 2022 to Revit 2024. This shall be at no additional cost to the Appointing Party and must be managed to minimize disruption to the project. Version updates may at the Appointing Party’s discretion occur less frequently, or by agreement of all stakeholders occur more regularly. Please note that the full year version is managed separately from service pack releases, these should be maintained at the most recent release. Task teams joining the project at a later date must remain in step with those already deployed, i.e. a task team joining the project in 2023 shall allow for a first full version update in 2024. Any update is to be explicitly agreed by the Appointing party, through consultation with each information manager.
